1. The 72-Hour Emergency Kit

When a Category 4 hurricane or severe supercell strikes, emergency services may be incapacitated for days. You must be self-reliant for at least 72 hours.

  • Water Storage: 1 gallon per person, per day. Prioritize heavy-duty jerry cans over flimsy plastic jugs.
  • Non-Perishable Food: High-calorie, nutrient-dense foods (MREs, protein bars, dehydrated camping meals).
  • Tactical Flashlights & Power: Hand-crank weather radios, waterproof flashlights, and high-capacity solar power banks.
  • First-Aid & Trauma: Tourniquets, pressure dressings, antiseptics, and a 30-day supply of prescription medications.

2. Fortifying Your Home Infrastructure

Wind damage is rarely the primary killer in severe weather; it is structural failure and subsequent flooding. Securing the envelope of your home is your first line of defense.

Install storm shutters or have 5/8" exterior grade plywood pre-cut for all windows. Reinforce garage doors, as their failure instantly pressurizes the house, blowing the roof off from the inside out. Always ensure your sump pumps have marine-battery backups.
